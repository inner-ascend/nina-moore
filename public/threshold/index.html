This is for women standing at the edge of who they are becoming — a vision pressing to be born, a self they are outgrowing. Visionaries, changemakers, artists-in-hiding, women returning to their own power after motherhood, rupture, or a long quiet.

§ The weekly rhythm

Three movements, every week.

Every circle moves through the same three movements, so the container breathes in a rhythm it can trust.

I · Open the portal

Open the portal

Live coaching and therapy. Nina works the field — surfacing the pattern that has hardened into identity, the thing standing at the threshold. Done in circle, so women witness each other.

Live · in circle ~45 min
II · Become the Wordsmith

Become the Wordsmith

Guided writing. The raw material is forged into a short portal poem — not pretty, alive. Written from the voice of the woman on the other side of the change.

Guided writing ~30 min
III · Speak it across

Speak it across

Ritual. The poem is read aloud and kept — a marker, proof she crossed. Between circles, a solo writing practice and a voice-note thread with Nina.

Ritual · spoken ~15 min

By week eight, she holds a book of portals — her own map of becoming, in her own words.

Eight weeks, eight portals.

The arc is the same for every woman, and entirely her own. We name the threshold, clear the voice, meet the shadow, befriend the not-knowing, find the spark, and cross — witnessed by women who saw her do it.

01 The Edge

Naming the threshold

OpenWhere exactly are you standing? What are you leaving, what is pulling? We give the formless change a shape.
WordsmithPortal poem of the Edge — a poem that names the doorway she stands at.
BetweenMorning pages at the threshold — three mornings of unedited writing.
02 The Tongue

Reclaiming your voice

OpenWhere did your voice go quiet? Whose voice are you writing in that isn't yours? We clear the throat.
WordsmithPortal poem of the True Voice — written with no one watching, no one to please.
BetweenWrite one sentence a day you'd never let anyone read.
03 The Descent

The shadow at the door

OpenThe pattern she keeps playing out, the fear under the threshold. Honest shadow work — Nina holds the floor of it.
WordsmithPortal poem of What I'm Putting Down — naming the weight, then choosing what she sets down here.
BetweenA letter to the part of me that is afraid.
04 The Valley

The not-knowing

OpenThe Valley of Becoming. How to stay in the in-between without rushing to resolve it. Uncertainty as fertile ground.
WordsmithPortal poem of the Open Question — she writes the question she is living, not the answer.
BetweenSit with one question all week. Write what it whispers.
05 The Spark ✦ midpoint

The gift wanting out

OpenCreative activation. The vision pressing to be born — the work, the art, the life she can feel but hasn't formed.
WordsmithPortal poem of the Spark — written from the creative fire, fast and wild, before the inner critic wakes.
BetweenMake one small "ugly" thing and tell no one whether it is good or bad.
06 The Vision

The woman on the other side

OpenWho is she — the woman becoming? Not a goal, a felt presence. We meet her, learn her yes and her no.
WordsmithPortal poem in Her Voice — written as the woman she is becoming, in present tense, as if already true.
BetweenOne day this week, move through an ordinary hour as her.
07 The Crossing

The deep, personal crossing

OpenA portal session for the deep crossing — held in circle, or privately one-to-one for those walking The Crossing.
WordsmithThe Crossing Poem — her central portal, the one she will return to for years.
BetweenRead the Crossing Poem aloud to herself daily until Week 8.
08 The Return

Witnessed

OpenShe returns to the circle changed. We close the container with intention.
RitualShe reads her book of portals aloud to the circle — the whole arc of her becoming, witnessed and sealed.
She leaves withThe bound book, her re-lit creative practice, and a sisterhood that knows her true voice.

§ Why this is Nina, and no one else

Most coaches do mindset. Most writing teachers do craft. Nina does the soul crossing the threshold, in language.

This braids the exact threads only she holds together: the therapist's and medicine-woman's depth, the weaver-of-words and guardian-of-dreams, the way of working that lives beyond definitions.

A category of one — writing as medicine, the page as the blank space we already are.

§ About · Nina Moore

A practice built slowly, in service of women crossing real ground.

Therapist, medicine-woman, weaver-of-words. I work with women at the point where one life is loosening and the next has not yet shown itself.

My practice weaves embodied awareness, intuitive insight, and grounded integration — and it lives in language, because some thresholds only reveal themselves in writing. I keep small containers by design, so the women who arrive are met with full presence.

She Who Becomes is the distilled form of that work: eight weeks, a small circle, and the portal poem as the door each woman walks through.

I work in English, Spanish, and French. Most letters are in English.
